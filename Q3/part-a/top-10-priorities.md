# Part A: Top 10 Organizations for Partnership Discussions

*Priority-ranked for QOSMIC's next 12 months. Ranking considers: urgency of need, likelihood of engagement (size match, geography, existing relationships), and strategic value.*

---

## 1. ISRO — Indian Space Research Organisation

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **One-line rationale** | Co-located in India, ISRO is developing optical communications for lunar and Earth observation missions, and a domestic partnership would give QOSMIC its anchor customer and credibility. |
| **Key decision-maker title** | Director, Human Spaceflight Centre or Director, UR Rao Satellite Centre (whoever oversees communications payloads) |
| **Likely engagement model** | Anchor tenancy / co-development. ISRO provides funding and mission requirements, QOSMIC builds and operates the Indian OGS network. Revenue: long-term service contract for downlink capacity on ISRO missions. |

**Why #1:** This is the most strategically valuable partnership for QOSMIC. An Indian space agency endorsement validates the technology, opens doors to other customers, and uses the home-country advantage. No other OGS provider is based in India — this is QOSMIC's unique positioning.

---

## 2. Planet Labs

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **One-line rationale** | 200+ EO satellites generating 150+ TB/day; RF downlink is their primary bottleneck and they've publicly acknowledged optical downlink as the solution. |
| **Key decision-maker title** | VP of Ground Systems and Operations |
| **Likely engagement model** | Service provider (OGS-as-a-Service). Planet pays per downlink session or per GB delivered, similar to their existing KSAT and SSC RF contracts. QOSMIC provides optical downlink capacity at a competitive price point. |

**Why #2:** Planet has the highest volume of downlink data among smallsat operators. They're already pushing the limits of RF. A successful demo with Planet would be a strong reference case.

---

## 3. ESA — European Space Agency

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **One-line rationale** | ESA is the most active agency in optical ground station development — funding the NODES network, integrating OGS for Direct-to-Earth, and seeking diverse geographic coverage (India is a gap in their network). |
| **Key decision-maker title** | Head of ESA-ESOC Ground Systems Engineering |
| **Likely engagement model** | Co-funded deployment. ESA provides grant or co-funding for an OGS at a QOSMIC site in India, in exchange for network access for ESA missions. This is how KSAT's Greek OGS was funded. |

**Why #3:** ESA has both the budget and the demonstrated willingness to fund OGS infrastructure (they funded KSAT's Greek station). Adding Indian coverage to the NODES network solves a real gap for them.

---

## 4. US Space Force / Space Development Agency

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **One-line rationale** | SDA's PWSA program is the largest funded optical communications program globally ($1.3B+), requiring ground segment support for the proliferated LEO constellation. |
| **Key decision-maker title** | Director, SDA or Program Manager, Ground Segment (PWSA) |
| **Likely engagement model** | Subcontractor to a prime contractor (e.g., Rocket Lab, Lockheed Martin, Northrop Grumman). QOSMIC provides OGS services as part of a larger prime contract. Not a direct customer — requires partnering with a US prime. |

**Why #4:** The budget is enormous and the need is real. However, QOSMIC would need a US prime partner and ITAR compliance. Worth pursuing with a US partner rather than directly.

---

## 5. Kepler Communications

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **One-line rationale** | Already demonstrated space-to-ground optical (May 2025) with Cailabs — has proven the use case and is scaling. Needs diverse geographic OGS coverage for their optical relay network. |
| **Key decision-maker title** | VP of Ground Segment or CTO |
| **Likely engagement model** | Network partner. QOSMIC adds an Indian OGS to Kepler's optical network. Kepler gets Indian coverage, QOSMIC gets utilization and revenue. |

**Why #5:** Kepler is further along than any other smallsat operator in optical. They need more ground stations. A partnership would be relatively low-risk since they've already proven the technology.

---

## 6. KSAT

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **One-line rationale** | Norway-based KSAT operates the world's first commercial OGS and manages ESA's Optical Nucleus Network — they're the most experienced OGS operator and a potential partner for network expansion into India. |
| **Key decision-maker title** | Director of Optical Communications or Head of Ground Network Services |
| **Likely engagement model** | Network partner / reseller. KSAT manages the global network, QOSMIC operates the Indian node. Revenue share on downlink services booked through KSAT's existing customer relationships. |

**Why #6:** Partnering with KSAT gives QOSMIC immediate access to ESA's network and existing customers. KSAT gets an Indian node without building one. Win-win.

---

## 7. French Ministry of Defence (via Definvest)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **One-line rationale** | Definvest invested directly in Cailabs — France is actively building sovereign optical comms capability. India-France strategic partnership (especially in space) provides a political channel. |
| **Key decision-maker title** | Director, Space Programs — Direction Générale de l'Armement (DGA) |
| **Likely engagement model** | Funded pilot. French MoD funds an OGS in India (potentially co-funded with ISRO) for secure downlink from French military satellites. QOSMIC operates the station. |

**Why #7:** India-France space cooperation is well-established (ISRO-CNES joint missions). This opens an alternative funding pathway outside traditional commercial channels.

---

## 8. Satellogic

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **One-line rationale** | Argentine EO operator with 50+ satellites and aggressive constellation expansion — RF downlink is already a constraint and they've explored optical alternatives. |
| **Key decision-maker title** | VP of Ground Segment |
| **Likely engagement model** | Service provider. Satellogic pays per downlink session. QOSMIC provides dedicated optical downlink capacity in India (covering the Indian Ocean region — a gap in their current network). |

**Why #8:** Satellogic has fewer satellites than Planet but higher per-satellite data volume (hyperspectral). They need diverse ground station coverage including the Indian Ocean region.

---

## 9. SSC — Swedish Space Corporation

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **One-line rationale** | SSC runs the NODES optical network (Chile, Australia) and is actively expanding — they're a direct competitor but also a potential partner for complementary geographic coverage. |
| **Key decision-maker title** | Head of Space Connectivity or Director of Ground Station Operations |
| **Likely engagement model** | Network partner / co-investor. SSC handles Americas and Australia, QOSMIC covers Asia/India. Joint marketing to shared customer base (satellite operators needing global coverage). |

**Why #9:** SSC is expanding aggressively. They have Western Australia and Chile. India/Asia is the next logical region. Instead of competing, QOSMIC can offer an Indian node.

---

## 10. ATLAS Space Operations

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **One-line rationale** | US-based GaaS company with $41M+ raised, software-defined ground network — and optical is a natural extension. ATLAS brings the software, QOSMIC brings the hardware. |
| **Key decision-maker title** | CEO or VP of Network Development |
| **Likely engagement model** | Technology partnership. ATLAS's Freedom software platform on QOSMIC's OGS hardware. Combined RF + optical offering. Revenue share. |

**Why #10:** ATLAS is the most software-forward ground station operator. Their model (software-defined network, easy onboarding) aligns with what QOSMIC would want to offer. A partnership lets QOSMIC focus on hardware while ATLAS handles the customer interface.
