# Q3: Business Operations & Strategic Execution

Optical ground station partnership landscape analysis and competitive benchmarking for QOSMIC SPACE.

## Contents

| Directory | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| `part-a/` | Partnership Landscape Analysis — 29 organizations mapped across 5 categories, top 10 ranked priorities, 3 cold outreach emails |
| `part-b/` | Competitive & Cost Benchmarking — 7 competitor profiles, OGS BoM cost estimate ($1.0-2.1M/station), market size analysis ($60M-$945M by 2030) |

## Part A: Partnership Landscape

**Files:**
- `landscape-map.md` — Global landscape of 29 organizations needing OGS services, categorized by type (satellite operators, space agencies, defense, telecom, ground station operators), geography, and urgency
- `top-10-priorities.md` — Ranked priority list for partnership discussions in the next 12 months, with rationale, decision-maker titles, and engagement models
- `outreach-emails.md` — 3 cold outreach emails (ISRO, Planet Labs, Kepler) under 150 words each

## Part B: Competitive & Cost Benchmarking

**Files:**
- `competitor-analysis.md` — 7 companies building/operating OGS (Cailabs, Mynaric/Rocket Lab, Tesat, KSAT, SSC, ATLAS, GA-Synopta) with stage, funding, and differentiation
- `OGS-cost-estimate.md` — Bill of materials for 50cm-class ground station with adaptive optics and fiber coupling. Total range: $1.0-2.1M/station
- `market-size.md` — Bottom-up market size estimate with methodology. Three scenarios: conservative ($60M), moderate ($360M), optimistic ($945M) by 2030
