## Summary

Cailabs is a French company that builds optical ground stations for laser satellite communications. Their OGS product line supports 10+ Gbps links and includes integrated atmospheric turbulence management using their Multi-Plane Light Conversion (MPLC) technology.

This reference documents the typical architecture of a modern optical ground station, including telescope, tracking mount, adaptive optics, fiber coupling, and receiver subsystems. It provides realistic specifications for each subsystem that inform our design decisions.

## Key Takeaways

- Modern OGS support 10+ Gbps laser downlinks from LEO satellites
- Integrated atmospheric turbulence management is a key differentiator for ground station vendors
- SDA (Space Development Agency) and CCSDS standards define the communication protocols
- A typical 50 cm class OGS includes: telescope, coarse pointing gimbal, fine steering mirror, adaptive optics, single-mode fiber coupling, and photon-counting receiver
