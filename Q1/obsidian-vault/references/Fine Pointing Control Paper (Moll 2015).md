# Fine Pointing Control for Optical Communications

## Bibliographic Information

- **Authors:** Moll, F.; Kuiper, S.; van der Harg, J.
- **Year:** 2015
- **Published in:** SPIE Security + Defence, Proceedings Vol. 9647
- **DOI:** 10.1117/12.2191102
- **URL:** https://elib.dlr.de/100883/

## Summary

This paper presents the design of a high-bandwidth fine steering mirror (FSM) for inter-satellite laser communication. The FSM uses piezoelectric actuators to achieve a first fundamental resonance mode exceeding 400 Hz while maintaining an actuation range of +/- 2.5 milliradians. The authors detail the mechanical design, control loop architecture, and measured performance.

The flexible hinge scheme allows the mirror to achieve the bandwidth needed to suppress tracking disturbances from satellite platform vibrations and atmospheric turbulence. The paper includes measured open-loop and closed-loop frequency responses showing >40 dB rejection at 100 Hz.

## Key Takeaways

- FSM bandwidth exceeding 400 Hz is achievable with piezoelectric actuators and flexible hinge designs
- The flexible hinge resonance is the primary bandwidth limitation
- Closed-loop tracking with over 40 dB rejection at 100 Hz is demonstrated
- Actuation range of +/- 2.5 mrad is sufficient for coarse pointing error correction
